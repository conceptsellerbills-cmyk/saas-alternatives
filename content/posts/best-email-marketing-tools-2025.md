# Best Email Marketing Tools 2025: Top Platforms for Growing Your List and Revenue

Email marketing generates an average ROI of $36 for every $1 spent — one of the highest returns of any marketing channel. But that ROI is heavily dependent on choosing the right platform for your specific use case. The best email marketing tool for a solo creator is completely different from the best tool for an e-commerce store or an enterprise marketing team.

This guide covers the best email marketing tools of 2025 across every audience type, with clear recommendations by use case.

## What to Evaluate in an Email Marketing Tool

**Deliverability** — Does your email actually reach the inbox? Deliverability is determined by your sending reputation, list hygiene, and the platform's shared infrastructure.

**Automation** — Can you build sophisticated sequences based on subscriber behavior (opens, clicks, purchases, site visits)?

**Segmentation** — Can you divide your list based on demographics, behavior, purchase history, and engagement?

**A/B testing** — Can you test subject lines, content, and send times to optimize performance?

**Landing pages and forms** — Does the platform include tools for growing your list?

**Integrations** — Does it connect to your CMS, e-commerce platform, CRM, and other tools?

**Analytics** — Can you understand what's working and why?

**Pricing model** — Contact-based (pay per subscriber) vs. email-based (pay per send volume) can dramatically affect cost at scale.

## Best Email Marketing Tools 2025

### 1. ActiveCampaign — Best for Automation and Sophisticated Marketing

ActiveCampaign combines email marketing with CRM and marketing automation in one platform. Its automation builder is the most powerful in the category — complex sequences based on any combination of triggers, conditions, and actions.

**Key strengths:**
- Visual automation builder with unlimited conditional logic
- Lead scoring and CRM integrated with email
- Site tracking (triggers automations based on pages visited)
- Predictive sending (sends at each contact's optimal time)
- Split automations for A/B testing sequences
- Extensive segmentation options

**Trade-offs:** More expensive than basic email tools; learning curve for automation complexity; interface can feel busy.

**Best for:** Mid-size businesses with sophisticated automation needs; B2B companies with sales and marketing alignment requirements.

**Pricing:** From $15/month (500 contacts) to custom enterprise.

### 2. Klaviyo — Best for E-Commerce

Klaviyo is purpose-built for e-commerce and dominates this category. Its Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce integrations are native and deep — Klaviyo pulls product, order, and customer data automatically to enable flows and segments that e-commerce requires.

**Key e-commerce features:**
- Abandoned cart sequences (the highest-ROI e-commerce automation)
- Browse abandonment flows
- Post-purchase sequences based on product purchased
- Win-back campaigns for lapsed customers
- Revenue attribution per email
- Predictive analytics (expected CLV, churn probability)

**Best for:** Any e-commerce business on Shopify, WooCommerce, or similar platforms.

**Pricing:** Free up to 250 contacts; scales with list size.

### 3. ConvertKit / Kit — Best for Creators and Newsletters

ConvertKit (recently rebranded as Kit) is the email platform built specifically for creators: bloggers, YouTubers, podcasters, course creators, and newsletter publishers. Its creator-centric features include:
- Commerce (selling digital products and subscriptions directly)
- Subscriber tags and segments built around creator workflows
- Visual automations designed for content-based sequences
- Clean, minimalist email editor perfect for text-first newsletters
- Creator network for cross-promotion

**Best for:** Solo creators, bloggers, newsletter writers, course creators, coaches.

**Pricing:** Free up to 10,000 subscribers (limited features); Creator at $25/month (1,000 subscribers).

### 4. Mailchimp — Best for Beginners and Small Businesses

Mailchimp is the most recognized name in email marketing and remains an excellent starting point for small businesses. Its interface is beginner-friendly, the template library is extensive, and the free tier is genuinely useful.

Recent improvements to automations and segmentation have closed the gap with competitors. The all-in-one marketing platform approach (website builder, landing pages, social ads) suits businesses wanting one tool.

**Trade-offs:** More expensive than competitors at scale; automation is still less sophisticated than ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo; deliverability reputation is mixed.

**Best for:** Small businesses starting with email marketing; beginners who want a familiar, well-supported platform.

**Pricing:** Free up to 500 contacts; Essentials from $13/month.

### 5. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — Best Budget Email Platform

Brevo prices by email sends rather than subscriber count — a significant advantage for businesses with large lists but lower send frequencies. A 10,000-contact list that sends monthly costs far less on Brevo than subscriber-count-based platforms.

Brevo includes transactional email, SMS marketing, and CRM alongside email campaigns in a single platform. The automation is solid, and the deliverability is reliable.

**Best for:** Businesses with large lists and lower send frequency; those who also need transactional email and SMS.

**Pricing:** Free up to 300 emails/day; Starter from $25/month (20,000 emails/month).

### 6. Beehiiv — Best for Newsletter Publishers

Beehiiv was built specifically for newsletter businesses and has grown dramatically in 2023–2025. It includes everything newsletter publishers need: subscriber management, email delivery, a custom newsletter website, referral program, ad network integration, and monetization tools.

The ad network allows newsletters to earn revenue from display and sponsored content ads without manually securing sponsorships. The referral program (readers earn rewards for referring subscribers) is native to the platform.

**Best for:** Newsletter publishers who want integrated monetization and a publication-first experience.

**Pricing:** Free up to 2,500 subscribers; Grow at $42/month; Scale at $84/month.

## Email Marketing Strategies That Drive Results

**Welcome sequences:** The highest-engagement moment with any subscriber is immediately after signup. A 3–5 email welcome sequence that delivers value and builds relationship dramatically outperforms one-off campaigns.

**Segmentation:** Email to your full list is the floor, not the ceiling. Segmenting by behavior (opened last 3 emails, clicked specific link, purchased specific product) and sending targeted content consistently outperforms blanket sending.

**Deliverability hygiene:** Clean your list regularly. Remove contacts who haven't opened in 6+ months (or run a re-engagement campaign first). Better engagement rates signal inbox placement quality.

**Subject line A/B testing:** Subject line is the most testable variable in email — test consistently and apply learnings.

**Plain text vs. HTML:** For newsletters and personal brands, plain text or minimal-design HTML often outperforms heavily designed templates because it feels more personal.

## Final Verdict

- **Best for sophisticated automation:** ActiveCampaign — the most powerful marketing automation available.
- **Best for e-commerce:** Klaviyo — purpose-built for Shopify and WooCommerce.
- **Best for creators:** ConvertKit/Kit — creator-specific features and pricing.
- **Best for beginners:** Mailchimp — familiar, well-supported, good free tier.
- **Best budget/high-volume list:** Brevo — send-based pricing is uniquely cost-effective.
- **Best for newsletters:** Beehiiv — publication-first platform with integrated monetization.

Match the tool to your use case, not the brand name. The best email marketing platform is the one that fits how your business actually grows and communicates.
