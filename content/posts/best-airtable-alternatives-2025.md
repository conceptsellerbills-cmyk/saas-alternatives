Airtable pioneered the spreadsheet-database hybrid category and remains influential, but its pricing has increased significantly while competitors have closed the feature gap. Many users find Airtable's per-user pricing expensive for collaborative workspaces, and the record limits on lower-tier plans frustrating for growing databases. With over 400,000 monthly searches, "best Airtable alternatives" reflects real demand for flexible, affordable database tools. This guide covers the best options in 2025.

## Why Consider Alternatives to Airtable?

**Pricing**: Airtable's free plan is limited to 1,000 records per base and 5 collaborators. Paid plans start at $20/seat/month, making it expensive for larger teams.

**Record limits**: Even on paid plans, record limits apply and can become restrictive for data-heavy applications.

**Performance**: Large Airtable bases can become slow. Performance degrades with complex automations and large record counts.

**Formula limitations**: Airtable's formula language is less powerful than traditional spreadsheet tools or true databases.

**Vendor dependency**: All your data in a proprietary cloud platform creates vendor lock-in risk.

## Best Airtable Alternatives 2025

### 1. Notion
**Best for teams that combine documentation and data**

Notion's database views (Table, Board, Calendar, Gallery, Timeline) provide similar functionality to Airtable's views, but integrated within a broader workspace that also includes docs, wikis, and linked pages.

**Key features:**
- Multiple database views
- Linked databases (relations between tables)
- Formula support
- Filters and sorts
- Rollup formulas
- Templates marketplace
- Free plan: 10 guests, unlimited blocks

**Pricing**: Free; Plus $10/user/month; Business $18/user/month

**Best for**: Teams that need both a database and a documentation workspace. If you're currently using Airtable + Notion separately, consolidating into Notion may make sense.

**Limitations**: Notion's database is less powerful than Airtable for pure data management — fewer field types, less complex automations, no native API filtering.

### 2. Baserow
**Best open-source Airtable alternative**

Baserow is an open-source Airtable alternative you can self-host. It closely mirrors Airtable's functionality with a clean interface and is actively developed.

**Key features:**
- Open-source (MIT license)
- Self-host for free (unlimited users, unlimited rows)
- Table, gallery, form, and kanban views
- Row and field-level permissions
- REST API for all tables
- Formula fields
- Airtable import

**Pricing**: Cloud from $5/user/month; self-hosted is free

**Best for**: Organizations that want Airtable-like functionality without per-user SaaS costs, or with data privacy requirements requiring self-hosting.

### 3. NocoDB
**Best for turning existing databases into Airtable-like interfaces**

NocoDB's unique value: it connects to existing SQL databases (MySQL, PostgreSQL, SQLite, SQL Server) and provides an Airtable-like spreadsheet interface on top. Instead of creating a new database, you bring your existing data.

**Key features:**
- Open-source
- Connects to existing SQL databases
- Multiple views (Grid, Gallery, Form, Kanban, Calendar)
- REST API auto-generated for all tables
- Role-based access control
- Self-hostable

**Pricing**: Cloud from $0 (forever free for small teams); self-hosted is free

**Best for**: Developers and technical teams who want a spreadsheet UI over existing databases. Unique use case vs. other alternatives.

### 4. Smartsheet
**Best for enterprise project management**

Smartsheet bridges traditional spreadsheet functionality with project management features, positioned at the enterprise market.

**Key features:**
- Sheet-based interface (more spreadsheet-like than Airtable)
- Gantt charts and timeline views
- Automated workflows
- Resource management
- Dashboards and reporting
- Enterprise security and compliance

**Pricing**: Pro $9/user/month; Business $19/user/month; Enterprise custom

**Best for**: Enterprise teams transitioning from Excel/Sheets who need project management features. Government and regulated industries where compliance matters.

### 5. Seatable
**Best affordable Airtable alternative with Airtable import**

SeaTable (Self-Hosted + Cloud) offers strong Airtable feature parity at lower prices. The cloud version offers a generous free plan (2,500 rows), and the enterprise self-hosted version is competitively priced.

**Key features:**
- Similar field types to Airtable
- Views: Grid, Gallery, Kanban, Calendar, Map
- Automations and API
- Python scripting for advanced operations
- On-premise / self-hosted option
- Airtable CSV import

**Pricing**: Free (2,500 rows per base); Plus $7/user/month; Enterprise from $23/user/month

### 6. Google Sheets + AppSheet
**Best for Google Workspace users**

For simpler use cases, Google Sheets combined with AppSheet (Google's no-code app builder) provides Airtable-like functionality within the Google ecosystem — with the added advantage of AppSheet's ability to turn the data into a mobile app.

**Pricing**: Google Sheets is free; AppSheet free plan available; paid from $10/user/month

**Best for**: Teams already deep in Google Workspace who want relational data functionality without leaving the ecosystem.

### 7. Fibery
**Best for product teams**

Fibery is a work management platform with a highly flexible data model — you can create custom entities and relationships that model your specific workflow. Popular among product and engineering teams as a combined backlog, roadmap, and documentation tool.

**Key features:**
- Highly flexible data model (define your own entities)
- Relations between any entity types
- Built-in docs (unlike Airtable)
- Roadmap and kanban views
- Automations

**Pricing**: From $10/user/month

**Best for**: Product teams who want a fully customizable work management tool that adapts to their process rather than requiring them to adapt to it.

## Airtable Alternative Selection Guide

| Use Case | Best Alternative |
|---|---|
| Documentation + database hybrid | Notion |
| Open-source, self-hosted | Baserow or NocoDB |
| Enterprise project management | Smartsheet |
| Google Workspace alignment | Google Sheets + AppSheet |
| Product/engineering teams | Fibery |
| Most affordable paid cloud | SeaTable |

## Conclusion

The right Airtable alternative depends on your primary use case. For most teams looking for an all-in-one workspace, **Notion** covers both documentation and databases at lower total cost. For those who want true Airtable feature parity without the pricing, **Baserow** (self-hosted) or **SeaTable** are the strongest technical alternatives. Technical teams connecting to existing databases should evaluate **NocoDB**.

Airtable remains excellent software — the question is whether its pricing is justified for your use case compared to these increasingly capable alternatives.
