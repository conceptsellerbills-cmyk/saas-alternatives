# Notion vs Confluence 2025: Which Wiki Tool Is Right for Your Team?

Notion and Confluence are the two dominant team wiki and knowledge management tools. Both help teams document, share, and organize knowledge — but they take fundamentally different approaches and serve different organizational needs.

Here's a complete comparison to help you choose.

## What Is Confluence?

Confluence (by Atlassian) is the enterprise wiki standard. Launched in 2004 and deeply integrated with Jira, it's the knowledge management tool of choice for software engineering teams and large enterprises. Pages, spaces, templates, and powerful macro system.

## What Is Notion?

Notion (launched 2018) is a flexible workspace that combines wiki, database, task management, and note-taking in a single tool. Its block-based editor and database system make it adaptable to almost any workflow.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Ease of Use

**Notion wins.** Notion's block editor is intuitive — click anywhere and start typing, add any element with a "/" command. New users can be productive within minutes.

Confluence has a steeper learning curve: page hierarchy, spaces, macros, and templates require more orientation. The editor has improved significantly in recent years but remains less intuitive than Notion.

### Features & Flexibility

**Notion wins for flexibility; Confluence wins for enterprise features.**

Notion's database system (filtered views, linked databases, rollups, formulas) is unique — no other wiki tool offers this level of structured data management integrated with documentation.

Confluence has more powerful macro library, better PDF export, compliance features (audit logs, data residency), and Space permissions models suited to large organizations.

### Jira Integration

**Confluence wins.** If you use Jira for project management, Confluence's Jira integration is deep and native — link Jira issues in pages, embed roadmaps, auto-update project status. Notion's Jira integration exists but is much shallower.

For engineering teams already using Atlassian tools: Confluence is the obvious choice.

### Search

**Confluence wins.** Enterprise-grade search with full-text content indexing, page hierarchy understanding, and CQL (Confluence Query Language) for power users.

Notion's search is improving but remains a common complaint — it doesn't search within databases as effectively and can miss content in nested pages.

### Pricing

**Notion wins for value.**

Notion: Free tier (up to 10 guests); $10/user/month Plus; $15/user/month Business.

Confluence: Free (up to 10 users); $6.05/user/month Standard; $11.55/user/month Premium.

For larger teams, Confluence becomes expensive quickly. Notion's pricing is simpler and often cheaper per seat.

### Real-Time Collaboration

**Comparable.** Both support simultaneous editing with real-time cursors. Notion's multiplayer mode is smooth. Confluence's real-time editing has improved significantly in recent years.

### Templates

**Notion wins for variety.** Notion has thousands of community-created templates for virtually any use case. Confluence's templates are more structured and business-focused but fewer in number.

### Integrations

**Confluence wins for enterprise.** As part of Atlassian, Confluence connects deeply with Jira, Trello, Bitbucket, and the entire Atlassian marketplace (over 3,000 apps).

Notion integrates well with Zapier, Slack, and common tools, but has fewer native enterprise integrations.

### Mobile Experience

**Notion wins.** Notion's mobile apps are well-designed and functional for reading and editing. Confluence's mobile apps are functional but less polished.

### AI Features (2025)

**Comparable, both improving.** Notion AI ($10/user/month add-on) generates content, summarizes documents, and answers questions across your workspace. Atlassian Intelligence does similar in Confluence with Jira-integrated context.

## When to Choose Confluence

- Your team uses Jira and needs deep project-documentation integration
- You're in a regulated industry needing audit logs, data residency, and compliance controls
- You're a large enterprise with complex permission hierarchies
- Your primary use case is software documentation and engineering wikis

## When to Choose Notion

- You want one tool for wiki + tasks + databases + notes
- Your team prioritizes ease of use and quick onboarding
- You need flexible database functionality alongside documentation
- You're a startup or smaller team where per-seat cost matters
- Your team's workflows span beyond engineering (marketing, ops, HR)

## The Hybrid Approach

Many teams use both: Confluence for engineering documentation (Jira integration), and Notion for everything else (marketing plans, HR docs, OKRs, meeting notes). This is more expensive but common in fast-growing companies.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I migrate from Confluence to Notion?
Yes — Notion provides a Confluence import tool that converts Confluence spaces to Notion pages. Tables and macros may not convert perfectly and require manual cleanup.

### Which is better for startups?
Notion. Easier to set up, better free tier, more flexible for non-technical teams, and the pricing is better for small teams.

### Is Confluence worth it without Jira?
Less so. Confluence's main advantage is Jira integration. Without it, Notion provides better value and a better user experience for most teams.
