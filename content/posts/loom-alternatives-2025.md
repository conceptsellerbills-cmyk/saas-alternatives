Loom popularized async video messaging for teams and has become synonymous with "quick screen recording with a talking head." But Loom's acquisition by Atlassian in 2023 and subsequent pricing changes have accelerated interest in alternatives. With over 450,000 monthly searches, "Loom alternatives" is a popular search among remote teams, educators, and content creators. This guide covers the best options in 2025.

## Why Consider Loom Alternatives?

**Pricing**: Loom's free plan was dramatically limited after the Atlassian acquisition — now restricting videos to 5 minutes and 25 videos per account. Paid plans start at $12.50/creator/month.

**Privacy concerns**: Some organizations prefer not to have internal communications stored on third-party servers.

**Storage limits**: Free and entry-level plans have limited storage for video archives.

**Feature gaps**: Some users need features Loom doesn't offer — advanced editing, more hosting options, or better team management.

## Best Loom Alternatives 2025

### 1. Screenpal (formerly Screencast-O-Matic)
**Best free alternative**

Screenpal offers a generous free plan with no watermarks and unlimited recording time (up to 15-minute segments). The tool covers both screen recording and video editing.

**Key features:**
- Free plan: unlimited recordings, 15-min per video, no watermark
- Screen + webcam recording
- Built-in video editor
- Cloud hosting and sharing links
- Captions (auto-generated on paid plans)
- Stock music library

**Pricing**: Free; Deluxe $3/month; Premier $6/month; Teams $8/user/month

**Best for**: Budget-conscious users who want Loom-like functionality without the cost. The free plan is genuinely useful.

### 2. Tella
**Best for polished async videos**

Tella is a screen recorder designed to make asynchronous videos look professional. It automatically adds clean backgrounds, smooth transitions, and captions, giving recordings a production quality that most screen recorders don't achieve.

**Key features:**
- Virtual backgrounds and video editing
- Auto-captions
- Zoom and cursor highlights
- Background blur
- Clean branding removal
- Video chapters

**Pricing**: Free (limited); Pro $19/month; Business $15/user/month

**Best for**: Customer success teams, sales, and anyone who needs async videos to look polished and professional.

### 3. Veed.io
**Best for video editing capabilities**

Veed is primarily a browser-based video editor with screen recording built in. It's stronger on the editing and production side than Loom, making it better for video content creators or anyone who needs more than simple screen shares.

**Key features:**
- Browser-based recording and editing
- Auto-subtitles (120+ languages)
- Text overlays, annotations, transitions
- Remove silence feature
- AI talking head avatar
- Brand kit customization

**Pricing**: Free (1GB storage, Veed watermark); Basic $18/month; Pro $30/month

**Best for**: Content creators who need both recording and editing in one tool, marketing teams creating polished async videos.

### 4. Claap
**Best for team collaboration on videos**

Claap focuses on collaborative async video — adding discussion features, video threading, and integration with project management tools to video sharing.

**Key features:**
- Screen + webcam recording
- Comment threads on video timeline (like Frame.io but lighter)
- Reactions
- Integration with Notion, Linear, Jira
- AI meeting recording + summaries
- Auto-chapters

**Pricing**: Free (10 clips); Pro $8/user/month; Business $12/user/month

**Best for**: Product and engineering teams who review work collaboratively through video comments — design reviews, code walkthroughs, product feedback.

### 5. Screenity
**Best completely free open-source option**

Screenity is a free, open-source Chrome extension for screen recording. No account required, no storage limits (videos save locally), no watermarks. It's the most no-strings-attached option.

**Key features:**
- Free, open-source Chrome extension
- Screen + webcam recording
- Annotations (draw on screen while recording)
- Blur tool for sensitive data
- Export as MP4 or GIF
- No cloud storage (downloads to your device)

**Pricing**: Completely free

**Best for**: Occasional users who don't want a subscription or cloud storage, anyone who needs local recordings only.

### 6. Jam
**Best for bug reporting and developer feedback**

Jam is specifically designed for developers and QA teams to record bugs and share them with developers. It automatically captures console logs, network requests, and device information alongside the screen recording — eliminating the "I can't reproduce it" problem.

**Key features:**
- Screen recording with automatic technical metadata capture
- Console logs attached automatically
- Jira, Linear, GitHub integration
- Browser devtools capture
- Desktop app available

**Pricing**: Free (25 Jams/month); Pro $10/month; Team $20/user/month

**Best for**: Development teams and QA engineers for bug reporting. Not a general-purpose Loom replacement, but exceptional for its specific use case.

### 7. Descript
**Best for video podcasters and content creators**

Descript is a video editor where you edit video by editing text. The audio transcription is the timeline — delete a word in the transcript and that audio/video is removed. Combined with screen recording, it's the most powerful editing-focused Loom alternative.

**Key features:**
- Edit video by editing transcript text
- Overdub AI voice cloning (re-record audio with your AI voice)
- Screen recording
- Auto-captions
- Studio Sound (AI audio enhancement)
- Filler word removal

**Pricing**: Free (1 hour transcription/month); Creator $12/month; Pro $24/month

**Best for**: Podcasters, course creators, and anyone who creates regular video content and needs editing capabilities alongside recording.

## Loom Alternative Decision Guide

| Use Case | Best Alternative |
|---|---|
| Free plan needed | Screenpal or Screenity |
| Polished customer-facing videos | Tella |
| Team collaboration on videos | Claap |
| Bug reporting in development | Jam |
| Video editing + recording | Veed.io or Descript |
| Complete privacy, local only | Screenity |

## Conclusion

The best Loom alternative depends heavily on why you're leaving Loom. For a direct feature-for-feature replacement at a lower price, **Screenpal** or **Tella** are the strongest choices. Teams that want collaborative video review should evaluate **Claap**. Users who need heavy editing alongside recording should look at **Descript** or **Veed**. And for anyone who just needs free, no-strings screen recording without cloud storage — **Screenity** can't be beaten.

The async video communication category continues to grow rapidly, and competition has driven better pricing and features across the board compared to Loom's current offering.
