# Best Shopify Alternatives 2025: Top eCommerce Platforms Compared

Shopify is the dominant eCommerce platform, but it is not the right choice for every business. Transaction fees on lower plans, rising subscription costs, and limitations in certain areas have pushed many merchants to explore alternatives. Here are the best Shopify alternatives in 2025.

## Why Look Beyond Shopify?

Shopify's strengths are real — it is easy to use, has an excellent app ecosystem, and handles most eCommerce needs well. But there are legitimate reasons to look elsewhere:

**Transaction fees**: Shopify charges 0.5% to 2% transaction fees unless you use Shopify Payments. For high-volume stores, this adds up significantly.

**Monthly costs**: Shopify's basic plan starts at $39/month. Advanced features require the $105 or $399 plans. Apps add additional costs quickly.

**Customization limits**: Shopify's Liquid templating language is less flexible than some alternatives. Deep customizations often require developer help.

**Pricing model changes**: Shopify has increased prices and changed its terms several times, creating uncertainty for merchants.

## Best Shopify Alternatives in 2025

### 1. WooCommerce (Best for WordPress Users)

WooCommerce is the most widely used eCommerce solution in the world, powering about 37% of all online stores. It is a free WordPress plugin that transforms a WordPress site into a fully functional store.

**Key features:**
- Free core plugin
- Unlimited products and categories
- No transaction fees (beyond payment processor fees)
- Thousands of free and paid extensions
- Full control over code and design
- Physical and digital products
- Subscriptions, memberships, bookings (via extensions)

**Pricing**: The WooCommerce plugin is free. You pay for hosting ($10 to $50/month), a theme ($0 to $200), and extensions for specific features ($0 to $200+ per year each).

The biggest advantage over Shopify is zero platform transaction fees and complete customization control. The trade-off is that WooCommerce requires more technical management — WordPress updates, security, backups, and hosting are your responsibility.

**Best for**: WordPress users, developers, businesses that want no platform transaction fees and full customization.

### 2. BigCommerce (Best for High-Volume Stores)

BigCommerce is Shopify's closest direct competitor and often wins for larger stores due to its no-transaction-fee policy and stronger built-in features.

**Key features:**
- No transaction fees on any plan
- More built-in features than Shopify (reducing app dependency)
- Multi-currency and multi-storefront
- B2B features on higher plans
- Headless commerce support
- Strong SEO tools

**Pricing**: Standard plan starts at $39/month. Plus at $105/month. Pro at $399/month.

BigCommerce's pricing mirrors Shopify but without transaction fees. For high-volume merchants paying Shopify's transaction fees, BigCommerce can be meaningfully cheaper at equivalent functionality. The built-in feature set is more comprehensive, which reduces the number of paid apps needed.

**Best for**: Growing stores with high revenue, B2B sellers, merchants who want to avoid transaction fees.

### 3. Wix eCommerce (Best for Simplicity)

Wix's eCommerce capabilities have improved dramatically and now cover most needs for small to mid-sized online stores, with the advantage of being significantly easier to use than Shopify.

**Key features:**
- Drag-and-drop visual editor
- 500+ customizable templates
- No transaction fees
- Product management, inventory, shipping
- Abandoned cart recovery
- Wix Payments built-in
- Multichannel selling (Instagram, Facebook, Google Shopping)

**Pricing**: Business plan starts at $17/month. Business Elite at $159/month.

Wix is noticeably easier to set up and customize than Shopify, particularly for non-technical users. The design flexibility is excellent. The trade-off is fewer third-party integrations than Shopify and less robust inventory management for very large catalogs.

**Best for**: Small businesses, service providers, stores with fewer than 500 products.

### 4. Squarespace Commerce (Best for Design-Forward Brands)

Squarespace is known for its design quality, and its eCommerce features have matured to the point where it genuinely competes for lifestyle brands, artists, and boutique retailers.

**Key features:**
- Beautiful templates optimized for product showcase
- No transaction fees on higher plans (0.9% to 3% on lower plans)
- Inventory management
- Digital products and subscriptions
- Abandoned cart recovery
- Email marketing built-in

**Pricing**: Basic Commerce at $36/month. Advanced Commerce at $65/month.

Squarespace stores look exceptional out of the box. For brands where aesthetics are central to the value proposition — fashion, jewelry, art, photography — Squarespace's templates are genuinely superior to what most Shopify themes deliver without significant customization.

**Best for**: Lifestyle brands, artists, boutique retailers, stores where visual presentation is critical.

### 5. Ecwid by Lightspeed (Best for Adding Store to Existing Site)

Ecwid is unique among eCommerce platforms — it is designed to be embedded into an existing website rather than replace it. You can add a full-featured store to any website, including WordPress, Wix, Squarespace, or custom-coded sites.

**Key features:**
- Embeds into any existing website
- Multichannel selling (Instagram, Facebook, Amazon, eBay)
- No transaction fees
- Inventory management
- POS integration
- Mobile app for store management

**Pricing**: Free plan (up to 10 products). Venture at $25/month. Business at $45/month. Unlimited at $105/month.

For businesses that already have a website and want to add eCommerce without rebuilding everything, Ecwid is the cleanest solution. The free plan with 10 products is genuinely functional for very small stores.

**Best for**: Businesses that want to add a store to an existing website without rebuilding it.

### 6. Sellfy (Best for Digital Products and Creators)

For creators selling digital products (ebooks, templates, courses, music, videos), Sellfy is purpose-built with simpler pricing than Shopify.

**Key features:**
- Digital products, subscriptions, and print-on-demand
- Storefront included
- No transaction fees
- Email marketing built-in
- Embeddable buy buttons for any website
- Upsells and discounts

**Pricing**: Starter at $22/month. Business at $59/month. Premium at $119/month.

Sellfy's pricing is based on your annual revenue, not per-seat or per-feature. For creators with moderate revenue, this is typically cheaper than Shopify for a comparable feature set focused on digital products.

**Best for**: Creators, coaches, and digital product sellers.

## Shopify vs Alternatives: Quick Comparison

| Platform | Transaction Fees | Starting Price | Best For |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| Shopify | 0.5-2% (without Shopify Payments) | $39/mo | General use |
| WooCommerce | None | ~$15/mo hosting | WordPress users |
| BigCommerce | None | $39/mo | High-volume stores |
| Wix eCommerce | None | $17/mo | Simple stores |
| Squarespace | None (Commerce plans) | $36/mo | Design-forward brands |

## Final Verdict

**WooCommerce** is the best alternative for WordPress users who want no transaction fees and full control. **BigCommerce** is the strongest direct Shopify competitor for growing merchants. **Wix** wins on ease of use for smaller stores. **Squarespace** is the right choice when design quality is the priority.

The best platform is the one that matches your technical capability, store size, and the features you actually need. Do not pay Shopify's transaction fees if you process significant volume — the alternatives have closed the feature gap substantially.
