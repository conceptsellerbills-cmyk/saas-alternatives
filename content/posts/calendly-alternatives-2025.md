Calendly popularized the automated scheduling link, but its pricing has increased significantly and competitors have caught up — or surpassed it — in features, flexibility, and value. With over 500,000 monthly searches, "Calendly alternatives" is a popular search among professionals who want scheduling automation without Calendly's recurring monthly cost or limitations. This guide covers the best alternatives available in 2025.

## Why Look for Calendly Alternatives?

**Pricing**: Calendly's paid plans start at $8/user/month and scale to $16/user/month for team features. For many users, this is a recurring expense that's hard to justify.

**Branding**: Free Calendly shows prominent Calendly branding on your booking pages. Removing it requires a paid plan.

**Limited customization**: Calendly's booking pages have limited customization compared to some alternatives.

**One-sided experience**: Calendly traditionally shows your available times without any consideration of the invitee's preferences (though they've added polling features).

**Integration limitations**: Some specific integrations or webhook customizations require higher-tier plans.

## Best Calendly Alternatives 2025

### 1. Cal.com
**Best free and open-source alternative**

Cal.com is the most direct Calendly alternative — open-source, with a cloud version and self-hosting option. It offers most of Calendly's core features in a cleaner, faster interface.

**Key features:**
- Free forever plan (unlimited event types for individuals)
- Unlimited event types without usage caps
- Round-robin and collective scheduling
- Zoom, Google Meet, Teams integration
- Routing forms (qualify leads before booking)
- Workflows (automated email/SMS reminders)
- Open-source (self-host for free)
- API access on all plans

**Pricing**: Free (individuals); Teams $15/month per team; Enterprise custom

**Best for**: Individuals and small teams who want full-featured scheduling without monthly fees. The self-hosted version is completely free.

**Why it stands out**: Cal.com has the closest feature parity to Calendly while offering a genuinely free cloud plan without the branding limitations.

### 2. TidyCal
**Best lifetime deal option**

TidyCal is a Calendly competitor focused on simplicity and value. It gained significant traction by offering a lifetime deal (one-time payment) on AppSumo, and remains one of the most affordable scheduling tools available.

**Key features:**
- Unlimited booking types
- Group bookings
- Redirect after booking
- Buffer time between meetings
- Zapier integration
- Clean, simple interface

**Pricing**: $19 one-time payment (lifetime deal); regular plans start at $8/month

**Best for**: Solopreneurs, freelancers, and coaches who want to eliminate monthly subscription costs permanently. The lifetime deal makes it a compelling one-time investment.

### 3. SavvyCal
**Best for respecting invitee preferences**

SavvyCal takes a different approach to scheduling — it shows invitees a calendar overlay where they can also see their own availability while choosing a time. This makes the experience more collaborative and considerate than traditional "here are my slots, pick one" tools.

**Key features:**
- Overlay scheduling (invitee sees their calendar + your availability)
- Collective scheduling (poll availability across multiple people)
- Ranked availability (invitees can rank preferred times)
- Custom branding
- Smart scheduling (learns your preferences)

**Pricing**: Scheduling Link $12/month; Full Access $20/month

**Best for**: Consultants, executives, and anyone who wants to make the scheduling experience feel more collaborative rather than one-sided.

### 4. Acuity Scheduling (by Squarespace)
**Best for service businesses**

Acuity is built for service businesses: coaches, consultants, therapists, fitness instructors, and any business with paid appointments. It combines scheduling with payment collection, intake forms, and client management.

**Key features:**
- Online payment collection (Stripe, PayPal)
- Intake forms (collect info before appointments)
- Packages and memberships
- Client management
- Reminder emails/SMS
- HIPAA compliance available

**Pricing**: Emerging $16/month; Growing $27/month; Powerhouse $49/month

**Best for**: Service businesses that need payment collection and client intake alongside scheduling.

### 5. Doodle
**Best for group scheduling and polls**

Doodle is the original group scheduling poll tool. Rather than sharing your availability, Doodle lets you propose multiple time slots and have participants vote on which works for them — ideal for scheduling meetings across multiple attendees with different schedules.

**Key features:**
- Meeting polls (everyone votes on preferred times)
- Bookable calendar (individual 1:1 scheduling link)
- Group meeting detection
- Zoom integration

**Pricing**: Free (with ads); Pro $6.95/user/month; Team $8.95/user/month

**Best for**: Scheduling meetings with 3+ external participants who need to find a mutually available time.

### 6. Zoho Bookings
**Best for Zoho ecosystem users**

Zoho Bookings integrates deeply with Zoho CRM, Zoho Meeting, and other Zoho products. For teams already using Zoho's suite, it's the natural scheduling choice.

**Key features:**
- Zoho CRM integration (auto-creates contacts and deals)
- Zoho Meeting integration
- Staff and resource scheduling
- Service catalog with pricing
- Payment collection

**Pricing**: Free (1 staff member); Basic $6/staff/month; Premium $9/staff/month

**Best for**: Companies using Zoho CRM and wanting native scheduling integration without third-party tool complexity.

### 7. Microsoft Bookings
**Best for Microsoft 365 users**

Microsoft Bookings is included in Microsoft 365 Business Standard plans. If your organization already pays for Microsoft 365, Bookings is essentially free.

**Key features:**
- Teams meeting integration
- Outlook calendar sync
- Staff scheduling and assignment
- Customizable booking page
- Reminder emails

**Pricing**: Included with Microsoft 365 Business plans

**Best for**: Organizations on Microsoft 365 who want a scheduling tool without additional cost.

## Conclusion

For most individual users and small teams, **Cal.com** is the strongest Calendly alternative — it matches nearly all of Calendly's features, has a genuinely free plan, and is open-source. Budget-focused users should look at **TidyCal's lifetime deal**. Service businesses with payment needs should evaluate **Acuity**. Teams using Microsoft 365 should explore **Microsoft Bookings** — it may be free for them already.

Calendly remains a polished, reliable tool, but its value proposition has weakened as competitors have caught up while offering better pricing.
