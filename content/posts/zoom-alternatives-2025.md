# Best Zoom Alternatives 2025: Top 8 Video Conferencing Tools

Zoom has 300 million daily meeting participants — but it's not the only option, and for many teams it's not the best one. Depending on your needs, there are alternatives that are cheaper, simpler, more private, or better integrated with your existing tools.

## Top 8 Zoom Alternatives

### 1. Google Meet — Best Free Option
Free for all Google accounts, included in Google Workspace. One-click meeting links, calendar integration, live captions, recording on paid plans. For teams already using Gmail/Calendar, there's almost no reason not to use Meet.
**Price:** Free (60-min limit for 3+ people); Workspace from $6/user/month | **Best for:** Google Workspace users

### 2. Microsoft Teams — Best for Microsoft 365
Video meetings, screen sharing, whiteboarding, breakout rooms, and recording — all included with Microsoft 365. For Microsoft-ecosystem organizations, Teams eliminates the need for a separate video tool.
**Price:** Included with M365 | **Best for:** Microsoft organizations

### 3. Whereby — Best for Simplicity
No app download required — fully browser-based. Custom room URLs that are always-on. The simplest video conferencing experience available.
**Price:** Free (45 min, 100 participants); $6.99/month for 200 participants | **Best for:** Client meetings, quick calls, non-technical participants

### 4. Loom — Best for Async Video
Not a live meeting tool — Loom lets you record video messages with screen capture. Reduces synchronous meeting load by enabling async communication.
**Price:** Free (5 min limit); $12.50/user/month | **Best for:** Async teams, replacing meetings with video updates

### 5. Jitsi Meet — Best Free & Open Source
100% open-source, browser-based video conferencing with no account required. Host your own instance for complete privacy, or use the free meet.jit.si server.
**Price:** Free | **Best for:** Privacy-focused teams, tech teams who can self-host

### 6. Webex — Best Enterprise
Cisco's enterprise video platform — excellent reliability, compliance features, hardware room system integration, and enterprise security.
**Price:** Free (40 min); $14.50/host/month | **Best for:** Large enterprises, regulated industries

### 7. Riverside — Best for Podcast/Recording Quality
Records each participant locally in high quality (up to 4K video, 48kHz audio). Automatically syncs for perfect post-production. The standard for podcasters and content creators.
**Price:** Free (2 hours/month); $19/month | **Best for:** Podcast recording, interview content, high-quality video production

### 8. Around — Best for Focus-Friendly Meetings
Floating heads design — small camera windows that stay at the edges of your screen. Designed to minimize disruption to your workflow during meetings.
**Price:** Free | **Best for:** Teams who multitask during meetings

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which Zoom alternative has the best free plan?
Google Meet (60 minutes for groups, unlimited 1:1), Jitsi (unlimited, no account), and Whereby (45 min) all offer strong free tiers.

### Is Google Meet as good as Zoom?
For most meetings: yes. Zoom has advantages in large webinars (up to 1,000 participants), more advanced host controls, and Zoom Phone integration. For everyday team calls, Google Meet is equivalent.

### What's the most private video conferencing tool?
Jitsi Meet self-hosted — you control all the infrastructure and no data passes through third-party servers. Jitsi.org's free service is also end-to-end encrypted.
