# Best Trello Alternatives in 2025: Kanban Tools Compared

Trello popularized kanban boards for project management, but its feature set has stagnated while competitors have advanced significantly. Trello's free tier limitations (one Power-Up per board, 10 boards per workspace) push growing teams toward alternatives that offer more at lower or equal cost.

## Why Consider a Trello Alternative?

- **Free tier limitations:** Trello's free plan caps boards at 10 and Power-Ups at 1 per board
- **Limited views:** Trello is primarily kanban-only; competitors add list, timeline, and calendar views
- **Automation limits:** Butler automation is powerful but limited in free tier
- **Price:** Trello Standard is $5/user/month — same as ClickUp's more feature-rich offering

---

## Best Overall Alternative: ClickUp

**Price:** Free (generous) | $7/user/month (Unlimited)

ClickUp is the most direct Trello upgrade. It offers kanban boards plus list view, Gantt chart, calendar, timeline, and workload views — all in the free tier. Spaces and Folders organize projects hierarchically in ways Trello's flat board structure cannot.

The free tier includes unlimited tasks, unlimited members, and multiple views — more than Trello's free tier provides.

**Best for:** Teams outgrowing Trello who want more views and organization without paying more.

---

## Best for Simplicity: Asana (Board View)

**Price:** Free (15 users) | $10.99/user/month (Premium)

Asana's Board view is a polished kanban implementation with the advantage of switching to list, timeline, or calendar view of the same tasks. The free tier is more capable than Trello's for project management (unlimited tasks, projects, messages, activity logs).

**Best for:** Teams that want kanban boards as one view option among many, within a more complete project management system.

---

## Best for Engineering Teams: Linear

**Price:** Free | $8/user/month (Standard)

Linear is a project management tool purpose-built for software teams. Its kanban/cycle view, GitHub integration, and performance (it's extraordinarily fast) make it the preferred Trello alternative for product and engineering teams. Automatic GitHub issue linking, PR status updates on tickets, and velocity tracking are native features.

**Best for:** Product and engineering teams who want a focused, fast tool designed specifically for software development workflows.

---

## Best Self-Hosted Free: Planka

**Price:** Free (self-hosted, open source)

Planka is an open-source Trello clone you can host on your own server. Its interface is nearly identical to Trello — boards, lists, cards, labels, due dates. If you have a server and basic technical capability, Planka provides unlimited Trello-like functionality at zero cost.

**Best for:** Privacy-first teams and those who want unlimited Trello functionality with complete data control.

---

## Best for Large Teams: Monday.com

**Price:** $9/seat/month (Basic)

Monday.com's board view competes with Trello's kanban, but its dashboard, automation, and cross-board reporting capabilities serve larger teams that need portfolio-level visibility.

**Best for:** Operations and project portfolio teams that need data across multiple boards in one view.

---

## When to Stay on Trello

Trello remains the right choice when:
- Your team has fewer than 10 boards and uses the free tier
- You need a simple visual kanban without project management complexity
- Your team is non-technical and prefers Trello's simplicity over more feature-rich alternatives
- Power-Up integrations you rely on are only available in Trello

---

## Final Recommendation

- **Outgrowing Trello's features:** ClickUp (more views, same or lower cost)
- **Need complete PM system:** Asana
- **Engineering team:** Linear
- **Want free + unlimited + private:** Planka (self-hosted)
- **Portfolio visibility:** Monday.com
