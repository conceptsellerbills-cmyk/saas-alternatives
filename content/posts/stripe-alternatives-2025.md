Stripe is the developer favorite for payment processing, but it's not always the right fit. High international payment complexity, strict fraud policies that can freeze accounts, and the complexity of tax compliance for SaaS products have driven many businesses to explore alternatives. With over 600,000 monthly searches, "Stripe alternatives" is a popular query among founders, developers, and e-commerce operators. This guide covers the best Stripe alternatives in 2025 for different use cases.

## Why Look for Stripe Alternatives?

**Account freezes and holds**: Stripe's fraud prevention is sophisticated but can result in legitimate businesses having funds held or accounts suspended with limited recourse. High-risk industries are particularly vulnerable.

**International payment complexity**: Stripe simplifies global payments compared to legacy processors, but international VAT/GST compliance, local payment method support, and currency handling remain complex.

**SaaS tax liability (Merchant of Record)**: US SaaS founders selling internationally face complex sales tax obligations. Stripe Billing handles payment but not tax compliance — you're still liable for collecting and remitting global sales tax.

**Pricing for lower volumes**: At less than $10,000/month in volume, Stripe's standard 2.9% + $0.30 is competitive. Some alternatives offer better rates at higher volumes or for specific business types.

**Feature gaps**: Stripe's subscription management and billing is powerful but requires significant development. Some alternatives provide better out-of-box subscription billing.

## Best Stripe Alternatives 2025

### 1. Paddle
**Best for SaaS companies (Merchant of Record)**

Paddle acts as the Merchant of Record (MoR) for your SaaS business — meaning Paddle handles global sales tax/VAT collection and remittance in over 200 countries. This eliminates one of the biggest operational headaches for SaaS founders: global tax compliance.

**Key features:**
- Merchant of Record (handles all global tax)
- Built-in subscription management
- Multiple pricing models (per-seat, usage-based, flat rate)
- Checkout overlay (embeds in your site)
- Dunning management for failed payments
- Revenue recognition and metrics dashboard
- Support for 200+ countries

**Pricing**: 5% + $0.50 per transaction (higher than Stripe, but includes tax handling)

**Best for**: SaaS companies selling globally who want to eliminate tax compliance liability. The higher rate is offset by not needing a dedicated tax professional or compliance software.

**Not ideal for**: Physical goods, high-volume transactions where rate matters more.

### 2. Lemon Squeezy
**Best for indie developers and creators**

Lemon Squeezy is the friendliest Merchant of Record platform for small digital product businesses — indie app developers, digital product creators, and bootstrappers. Simple to set up, handles all global tax, and includes email marketing and affiliate management.

**Key features:**
- Merchant of Record (global tax handled)
- Digital product delivery
- License key management
- Subscription billing
- Affiliate program management
- Email marketing (simple but functional)
- Free plan for small volumes

**Pricing**: 5% + $0.50 per transaction (similar to Paddle)

**Best for**: Indie developers selling software licenses, digital downloads, and SaaS products. Significantly easier to set up than Paddle or Stripe.

### 3. PayPal / Braintree
**Best for buyer familiarity and marketplaces**

PayPal remains the most recognized payment brand globally. Despite developer frustration with its API, PayPal's buyer conversion rate is often higher than alternatives because of consumer trust and the PayPal wallet balance.

**Braintree** (PayPal's developer platform) offers a much better API experience with support for cards, PayPal, Venmo, Apple Pay, and Google Pay.

**Pricing**: 2.89% + $0.49 per transaction (PayPal standard); Braintree: 2.59% + $0.49 for cards

**Best for**: Consumer-facing businesses where PayPal trust increases conversions, marketplaces that need split payments, international businesses where PayPal is the preferred local method.

### 4. Square
**Best for in-person and omnichannel retail**

Square dominates the in-person POS market and has extended to online payments, invoicing, and subscription billing. If you have both in-person and online transactions, Square's unified ecosystem is hard to beat.

**Key features:**
- Free card reader for in-person payments
- Online store builder
- Invoicing
- Appointment scheduling
- Payroll integration
- Inventory management

**Pricing**: 2.6% + $0.10 in-person; 2.9% + $0.30 online

**Best for**: Retail stores, restaurants, service businesses with both in-person and online sales. Not a natural fit for SaaS or digital products.

### 5. Authorize.net
**Best for established businesses needing fraud tools**

Authorize.net (owned by Visa) is one of the oldest payment gateways, with enterprise-grade fraud detection tools and high-volume rate negotiation. Less developer-friendly than Stripe, but more established with certain merchant categories.

**Pricing**: $25/month + 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction

**Best for**: Established businesses processing high volumes who need dedicated fraud management and rate negotiations.

### 6. Chargebee
**Best subscription management layer over Stripe**

Chargebee sits on top of payment processors (including Stripe) and adds sophisticated subscription management, revenue recognition, dunning, and billing logic. If you want Stripe's infrastructure with better subscription capabilities, Chargebee is the answer.

**Key features:**
- Advanced subscription billing (trials, addons, usage-based, tiers)
- Revenue recognition (ASC 606 compliance)
- Dunning management with smart retries
- Customer portal
- Revenue analytics

**Pricing**: Starts at $299/month; scales with revenue

**Best for**: SaaS companies at $100K+ MRR who need sophisticated subscription management and revenue reporting.

### 7. Mollie
**Best for European businesses**

Mollie is the leading payment processor for European businesses, supporting all major local European payment methods: iDEAL (Netherlands), Bancontact (Belgium), SOFORT (Germany), Klarna BNPL, and more.

**Pricing**: Pay-per-transaction, no monthly fee; rates similar to Stripe

**Best for**: European-based businesses that need local payment method support beyond card payments.

## Choosing the Right Alternative: Decision Framework

**Selling SaaS globally and want tax handled for you?** → Paddle or Lemon Squeezy

**Need consumer trust signals and PayPal wallet buyers?** → PayPal/Braintree

**In-person + online omnichannel?** → Square

**European business needing local payment methods?** → Mollie

**Need advanced subscription billing on top of Stripe?** → Chargebee

**High risk or high volume with negotiable rates?** → Authorize.net

**Most developers/startups without specific needs?** → Stripe remains the best default

## Conclusion

Stripe is still the best general-purpose payment processor for developers in 2025. But for SaaS companies dealing with global tax compliance, Paddle and Lemon Squeezy's Merchant of Record model is transformative — the higher transaction fee is more than justified by eliminating tax complexity. For consumer e-commerce, Square or PayPal may increase conversions. For European local payment methods, Mollie fills gaps Stripe doesn't address as well.

The right choice depends heavily on your business model, customer geography, and operational priorities.
